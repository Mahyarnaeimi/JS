Mahyar Naeimi


I grew up in a family that has deep roots in various forms of fine arts such as painting, miniature and illustration, sculpture, fashion, and media arts. I had the chance to develop my creativity and imagination as a valuable authority, which I profit from to execute artistic ideas.

After nine years of working in marketing, advertising, and graphic design related roles, I decided to embrace a new challenge in my career and pursue my passion for game art and especially character design.

I am passionate about the protection of our planet, the environment, and animals. My goal is to always try to have positive impacts on the environment and the community I live in. I believe that life is an amazing learning opportunity and I look at every moment of life as a valuable chance to learn from the people as well as the challenges that I face.

More about me





  • 3D Knowledge

    Almost 2006, before I started studying medical engineering at university, I started working as a graphic designer, that's why I started learning design software, especially Corel Draw and Photoshop. My constant interest in animation and video games, especially my enthusiasm for character design and modeling, led me to start learning ZBrush, 3dsMax, and Maya with their capability to reach excellent final works such as XGen, Arnold, VRay. At the same time, I became self-taught and, following the workflow of leading artists in the industry to become acquainted with the professional production pipeline and the accurate stages of UV, texturing to deliver excellent material and shading for render of my works with UVLayout, Substance Painter. Recently, I've gone a step further and started learning Marvelous Designer to get the best out of my concepts.

  • MY STORY BEGINS HERE:

BuboX Robot :A recreation of an old concept design. I was inspired by the charisma and glory of the Bubo Owl. This project was developed by the knowledge of design, materialize, and lighting of hard surfaces using ZBrush and Maya to compose a blackout and retopology the mesh to reach a perfect optimization, which made it prepared for UV. The texturing and render set has proceeded with the help of Substance painter and Marmoset.


Bumpy Clown :This clown was illustrated and then created with the technique of random silhouette. I decided to turn it into a 3D character as a challenging model, especially because of its curly hair, in which I used Ornatrix instead of XGen this time.
